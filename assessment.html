Section 06

Assessment

Assessments are conducted in several ways, checking and developing historical thinking both while learning happens and at the end of a unit.

Within lesson assessment

The aim of within lesson assessments is to check and develop understanding of historical thinking while the learning is happening. This can take many forms, such as questioning, reviewing outcomes in books, and a low stake quiz.

End of unit assessments

The aim of an end of unit assessment is to assess children’s understanding of the core disciplinary and substantive knowledge through the lens of a disciplinary concept. For many children, the structure of an end of unit assessment is similar to the structure of a final piece of work within an English unit. However, for those where writing is a barrier, children demonstrate their knowledge in a variety of ways. Examples include voice notes, dictation, or mind maps.

Assessments are normally conducted over 2 lessons or 1 afternoon, with children analysing a new source. Prompts or scaffolding are provided from other previously seen sources. Children are given time to plan and draft their response.

Following an end of unit assessment, we explore:

  • Which children are working below ARE and what support is needed to enable all children to achieve?
  • What responses would we expect for pupils who are ARE or GD?
  • How closely does our assessment align with the outcomes specified on the planning?
  • Did the source allow children to showcase both their substantive and disciplinary knowledge?
  • Did the assessment allow children to provide enough depth in their response?
