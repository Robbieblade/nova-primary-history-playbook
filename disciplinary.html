Section 02

Disciplinary Concepts

We explicitly teach six disciplinary concepts so that history becomes something pupils can think with, not just facts to recall. Each unit concentrates on one or two; others may be referred to within individual lessons.

Cause and ConsequenceFactors or events that lead to a particular outcome and the result or change that follows.
Change and ContinuityElements that stay the same versus shifts and transformations.
Similarity and DifferenceMoving beyond stereotypical assumptions.
EvidenceAny factual information, artefact or source that historians analyse.
InterpretationAnalysing, explaining and constructing narratives about past events using evidence.
SignificanceThe impact an event had at the time and its lasting legacy today.

Progression of Disciplinary Knowledge

Key disciplinary knowledge has been identified for each year group/phase, to ensure clear progression is in place. This is outlined on each school’s progression documents. In order to ensure there is appropriate challenge within each unit, during the planning stage, this disciplinary knowledge is further broken down and specified, using solo taxonomy to support progression.

Disciplinary knowledge is taught in equal measure to substantive knowledge, as without it, pupils would be limited to “everyday thinking,” restricting their ability to make valid historical inferences. Individual learning intentions therefore are a balance between both the disciplinary and substantive knowledge.
