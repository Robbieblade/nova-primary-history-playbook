Section 04

Sources

Sources serve as the fundamental building blocks of historical enquiry. They provide the hinterland detail that makes core knowledge memorable and ‘sticky’ and support chronological awareness through dated artefacts and images. Pupils learn that historians use sources to construct claims, arguments, and accounts, and that any historical judgement must be underpinned by rigorous evidence.

Our curriculum ensures that pupils encounter a diverse range of sources within a rich framework of substantive knowledge. Each source that’s been selected has a clear purpose, has guided, rather than open ended questions, and adults model how historians would interpret them. As a result, sources are integral to the majority of our lessons and allow pupils to refine their questioning skills and develop curiosity.
