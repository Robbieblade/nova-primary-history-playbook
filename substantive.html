Section 03

Substantive Concepts

Substantive concepts help form the rationale for each unit of work. Each Nova school has autonomy over which substantive concepts they teach, taking into consideration their local context.

Examples

Hierarchy & Power Conflict Community Invaders & Settlers Legacy Equality Innovation & Invention

Why we plan around recurring concepts

These substantive concepts form the basis for the substantive knowledge that is taught within each unit of work. The rationale for this is to support children to understand, organise, and remember complex historical information across different time periods. By planning units of work around these recurring concepts, it prevents history from becoming episodic or disconnected. It also further supports children to move beyond isolated facts.

For example, concepts such as hierarchy & power enable us to connect contrasting units into a coherent narrative, while also representing the complexity of human experience. By exploring concepts like community across different global contexts, pupils can develop an informed respect for their own identities and those of others. The substantive knowledge that is taught in each history unit is outlined on each school’s progression documents.
