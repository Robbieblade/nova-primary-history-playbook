Section 01

Vision & Intent

The National Curriculum – Purpose of Study

A high-quality history education will help pupils gain a coherent knowledge and understanding of Britain’s past and that of the wider world. It should inspire pupils’ curiosity to know more about the past. Teaching should equip pupils to ask perceptive questions, think critically, weigh evidence, sift arguments, and develop perspective and judgement. History helps pupils to understand the complexity of people’s lives, the process of change, the diversity of societies and relationships between different groups, as well as their own identity and the challenges of their time.

Our Vision

At Nova, our vision for history is rooted in pupils becoming expert historians through a rich, rigorous dialogue with the past. Our history curriculum is not simply a collection of facts, but a process that enables pupils to think critically, ask perceptive questions, and understand that history is constructed, interpreted and debated. We aim to inspire a lifelong curiosity and for every pupil to challenge narratives using our shared principles of historical enquiry. We aim to remove barriers for all learners, through making adaptations, rather than diluting historical complexity.

Our Intent

We intend for all pupils to understand the history of Britain and the wider world as a coherent narrative, from the earliest times to the present day. We explore local, regional, and global contexts, and intend for pupils to contextualise their own identities and develop an informed respect for the identities of others.

We base our units around the mastery of substantive concepts (such as hierarchy & power, conflict, and community), ensuring they are revisited in increasingly complex contexts. We challenge misconceptions through rich “hinterland” stories to overcome generalisations and provide a more inclusive representation of the past.

We explicitly teach six disciplinary concepts (cause & consequence, change & continuity, similarity & difference, evidence, significance, and interpretation) in order to transform history to something meaningful and engaging for each child.
